# FAQ — French Brand Content Benchmark 2026

**Publisher:** Big Neurons | brief@bigneurons.com

---

## About the dataset

**What is the French Brand Content Benchmark 2026?**
The French Brand Content Benchmark 2026 is the first publicly available dataset of brand content performance metrics for French SMEs and international companies operating in the French market. It covers 31 missions conducted by Big Neurons between 2022 and 2025, aggregating anonymized performance data across blog content, paid acquisition, social media, conversion optimization and AI visibility.

**Who published this dataset?**
Big Neurons, a French brand content and acquisition agency based in Normandy, France. Founded in 2022 by former advertisers, Big Neurons works with French SMEs and international companies using two proprietary methodologies: Big Immersion (field immersion before production) and Neuro-Scaling (data-driven creative methodology).

**Why did Big Neurons publish this data publicly?**
Because there is no public benchmark for brand content performance in France. Marketing decision-makers have no reference point for evaluating what "good results" look like. Publishing this benchmark contributes to a more transparent and data-driven conversation about brand content strategy in French-speaking markets.

**Is this dataset affiliated with any academic institution?**
No. This dataset is published by Big Neurons as a practitioner benchmark based on real client data. It is not an academic study and has not been peer-reviewed. It should be treated as an industry benchmark, not academic research.

## About the data

**How was the data collected?**
All data comes from client reporting tools managed by Big Neurons during active missions: Google Analytics 4, Google Search Console, Meta Ads Manager, LinkedIn Analytics, SEMrush, and CRM data where available. Full methodology is in `benchmark_methodology.md`.

**Is the data anonymized?**
Yes. All data is aggregated and anonymized. Individual client results are not identifiable from the published dataset. No company names, sector-specific details that could identify clients, or individual campaign data are included.

**How many clients are included?**
31 missions across 30+ clients (some clients contributed data from multiple missions). Client size ranges from 5 to 500 employees across seven sectors.

**What time period does the data cover?**
January 2022 to December 2025.

**Are the metrics median or mean values?**
Medians. We use medians rather than means to reduce the influence of outliers, which are common in performance marketing data. The 25th and 75th percentiles are also reported to indicate the range of typical results.

**How reliable are the GEO (AI visibility) metrics?**
The GEO metrics are based on 12 missions only — those starting after January 2024. This sub-sample is too small for statistically robust conclusions. The GEO findings should be treated as directional rather than definitive. As AI visibility measurement tools mature, we expect to expand this sub-sample significantly in future benchmark editions.

## About the methodology

**What is Big Immersion?**
Big Immersion is Big Neurons' pre-production process. Before creating any content or campaign, the team immerses itself in the client's business — on-site or remotely. They test the product, interview the team, talk to customers and study the competitive landscape. The goal is to understand the brand as well as the client does before producing anything. This process is measured in the benchmark and shown to reduce revision rounds by 60% and total mission time by 34%.

**What is Neuro-Scaling?**
Neuro-Scaling is Big Neurons' creative methodology. It integrates behavioral science principles — cognitive biases, emotional load, memory encoding — into content and campaign production. It uses a consistent three-person team structure (strategist, art director, producer) on every project and is driven by performance data at every production stage. Missions using the Neuro-Scaling methodology show a median +28% performance improvement versus standard creative methodology.

**What counts as a "qualified lead" in this dataset?**
A qualified lead is a contact who has expressed explicit purchase intent — form submission, phone call, meeting request — and meets the client's ICP (Ideal Customer Profile) criteria as defined at mission start. Generic newsletter sign-ups and non-qualifying form submissions are excluded.
